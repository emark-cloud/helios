# Backtest — `yield_rotation_v1` reference strategy (90 days, synthetic)

Closes Phase 2 acceptance gate **"Backtest reports for each reference
strategy committed under `docs/backtests/<class>_90d.md`"**
(`TODO.md` line 301), now driven through the WS4 SDK
`run_yield_backtest` engine instead of a bespoke harness.

## How this report is produced

WS4 lifted the YR backtest driver into the SDK at
`helios.backtest.run_yield_backtest`. The driver:

1. Walks a list of `{market_id: YieldTick}` snapshots one tick at a
   time.
2. Calls `strategy.on_yield_tick(snapshot)` and records any returned
   `RotationIntent` (its `m_from` is checked against the strategy's
   `active_market` after the first deployment to catch phantom
   rotations).
3. Accrues APY of the active market into `realized_yield_usd` per
   tick interval, separately from a `gross_yield_usd` tally that
   excludes bridging costs.
4. Charges a flat per-rotation bridging cost (`bridging_cost_bps ×
   initial_capital`) against `realized_yield_usd` so the user-facing
   P&L reflects a real frequently-rotating cost picture.

Third-party YR strategies whose constructors take defaults can run the
driver via `helios backtest --strategy …/strategy.py --period 90d`.
The reference impl requires `allowlisted_markets` at construction
time, so this report is generated by
[`docs/backtests/_yield_rotation_v1_harness.py`](_yield_rotation_v1_harness.py),
which is now a thin wrapper that constructs the reference strategy
with non-default args and delegates to `run_yield_backtest`.

## Strategy summary

- **Class:** `yield_rotation_v1` — Poseidon-Merkle-attested rotation
  between two allowlisted lending markets when the destination's APY
  exceeds the source's by `signal_threshold + bridging_cost` bps.
- **Asset universe:** N/A. YR doesn't hold spot assets; it holds
  positions in money-market vaults keyed by `market_id` (uint64).
- **Operator-tunable params:** `allowlisted_markets` (required),
  `signal_threshold_bps` (default 80), `bridging_cost_bps` (default 30),
  `position_fraction` (default 0.5).
- **Hard caps:** `max_position_size_usd = 25_000`,
  `fee_rate_bps = 1_500` (15% perf fee — tighter than directional
  classes, which charge 2_000 bps).
- **Circuit invariants enforced by `yield_rotation_v1.circom`:**
  Poseidon-Merkle membership of `(m_from, apy_from)` and
  `(m_to, apy_to)` against `yield_oracle_root` (depth 6),
  Poseidon-Merkle membership of both `m_from` / `m_to` against the
  strategy's private allowlist root (depth 4) bound through
  `trade_hash`, `apy_to − apy_from ≥ signal_threshold + bridging_cost`,
  `m_from ≠ m_to`, `amount_rotating > 0`.

## Harness setup

- **Markets:** 4 allowlisted (`market_id ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4}`) with
  base APYs `{380, 420, 510, 290}` bps.
- **Tick cadence:** hourly → `90d × 24 = 2160` ticks.
- **APY trajectory per market:** `base + slow_drift + noise + jump`
  where `slow_drift` is a 14-day sinusoid, `noise ∈ [−15, +15]` bps,
  and `jump` fires on ~16% of ticks at ±50–100 bps.
- **Initial capital:** $50,000.
- **Strategy params:** `signal_threshold_bps = 80`,
  `bridging_cost_bps = 30` ⇒ rotation requires a ≥ 110 bps APY
  improvement net of bridging.

## Results across five seeds

| Seed | Rotations | Avg active APY (bps) | Median diff (bps) | Realized yield ($) |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 17   |  9 | 511.4 | 139.0 |  −719.55 |
| 42   | 12 | 509.8 | 123.5 | −1,171.46 |
| 101  |  6 | 510.1 | 124.5 |   −271.16 |
| 314  |  7 | 511.9 | 121.0 |   −418.87 |
| 7331 |  3 | 512.0 | 123.0 |    181.24 |
| **median** | **7** | **511.4** | **123.5** | **−418.87** |

What these numbers signal:

- Average active APY converges to **~511 bps** across all seeds — the
  base APY of market 3, the highest in the allowlist. Confirms
  `max(candidates, key=apy)` market selection routes capital to the
  dominant market and stays there absent a spread-clearing alternative.
- Median rotation differential of **123 bps** is close to the floor
  (`signal_threshold + bridging_cost = 110`); the strategy doesn't
  wait for huge spreads. Every executed rotation has a positive APY
  differential ≥ 110 bps — confirms the in-strategy gate matches the
  on-chain circuit constraint.
- **Realized P&L is mostly negative for the default fee schedule.**
  Gross yield of ~511 bps over 90 days on $50k ≈ $629; with 7-12
  rotations at $150 each (30 bps × $50k), bridging costs of
  $1,050–$1,800 burn through gross. Only seed 7331 (3 rotations) is
  net-positive. **This is a feature of the test, not a bug** — it
  surfaces that the reference YR strategy is *over-rotating* against
  the synthetic noise floor, which is exactly the kind of insight an
  operator should see before publishing live.
- A correctly-tuned operator either widens `signal_threshold_bps`
  (rotate less), narrows the allowlist (less candidate noise), or
  trims `position_fraction` (smaller bridges per rotation). The
  three knobs are the operator's lever; the on-chain constraint
  permits any of them.

## What this report does *not* cover

- **Bridging time / multi-block confirmation.** The harness assumes
  instant rotation. The on-chain path (Phase 5 cross-chain LZ
  delivery) carries its own latency; per `Helios.md §10.4`, that's
  what `bridging_cost_bps` is meant to amortise.
- **Compounding.** Realized yield is accrued linearly against the
  initial capital, not the running NAV. WS4 PR 3/3 introduces NAV-
  based sizing for directional classes; YR compounding is a future
  Phase 4+ deliverable.
- **Real-market alpha.** Synthetic APY traces; operators must re-run
  against real money-market data they trust.
